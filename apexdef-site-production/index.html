Straight answers.

What is DEF and does my vehicle need it?
DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid, also called AUS 32) is a 32.5% urea solution injected into the exhaust of SCR-equipped diesels. If your vehicle is a BS-VI truck/bus/car, a TREM IV tractor above 50 HP, a CEV IV machine or a CPCB IV+ genset — it needs DEF to run legally and at full power.
How much DEF will I use?
Typically 3–6% of your diesel consumption — about 5 litres of DEF per 100 litres of diesel for a BS-VI truck. High-load agricultural duty runs at the upper end.
What happens if I run out?
BS-VI vehicles warn, then derate — power and speed are cut by the ECU until you refill. Don't test it; keep a can in the cab.
How long does DEF last in storage?
12–18 months under proper Indian storage — sealed pack, under 30 °C, away from sunlight. Every APEX pack carries its manufacture and expiry date.
