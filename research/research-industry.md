# DEF / AdBlue / AUS 32 — India Industry Research Digest
Compiled 2026-07-16. Verified via web search + source fetches. Flags: ⚠️ = verify before publishing as fact.

## 1. Standards & Certification

### ISO 22241 (international standard family for AUS 32)
| Part | Title / Coverage |
|---|---|
| ISO 22241-1 | Quality requirements — chemical spec of the 32.5% urea solution (the "spec table") |
| ISO 22241-2 | Test methods — lab methods for each parameter (Annexes B–I) |
| ISO 22241-3 | Handling, transportation and storage — incl. shelf-life table, materials compatibility |
| ISO 22241-4 | Refilling interface — for commercial vehicles/buses >3.5 t GVW (stationary dispensing) |
| ISO 22241-5 | Refilling interface for passenger cars (bottles/refill systems) |

- Product = 32.5% (nominal) high-purity technical urea + 67.5% demineralised/deionised water. 32.5% is chosen because it is the eutectic composition (lowest, sharpest freezing point, −11°C).

### BIS / IS 17042 (India)
- Indian standard: **IS 17042 (Part 1)** "Diesel Engines — NOx Reduction Agent AUS 32 — Part 1: Quality Requirements" — technically equivalent to ISO 22241-1. **IS 17042 (Part 2):2020** = test methods, identical (dual-numbered) to ISO 22241-2:2019.
- **BIS certification IS MANDATORY.** Quality Control Order: "Diesel Engines – NOx Reduction Agent AUS 32 (Quality Control) Order, 2024", notification S.O. 922(E) dated 26/27 Feb 2024, issued by Dept. of Chemicals & Petrochemicals, Ministry of Chemicals & Fertilizers. In force **180 days after publication ⇒ ~late August 2024**. Applies to domestic manufacture AND imports; exempts export-only goods. BIS is certifying + enforcing authority; product must bear the **ISI Standard Mark** under a BIS licence (Scheme-I, Schedule-II, BIS Conformity Assessment Regulations 2018). Selling without the licence = seizure/penalties.
- ⚠️ Sources cite the referenced standard variously as IS 17042 (Part 1):2018 and :2020 — confirm the current version on the BIS portal before printing a year on the site. Safe wording: "BIS certified to IS 17042 (Part 1)".

### AdBlue trademark (critical legal point)
- **AdBlue® is a registered trademark of VDA** (Verband der Automobilindustrie e.V., German Association of the Automotive Industry). Registered in India as well (VDA publishes a country trademark list).
- Use of the name "AdBlue" requires a **VDA licence**, granted only after passing an ISO 22241-based quality-management audit by VDA-appointed auditors. Licensees may sub-license trading partners. VDA publishes a public licensee list (Indian companies e.g. MetaBlue appear on it).
- An unlicensed Indian brand **may NOT** brand or market its product as "AdBlue". Safe, legal descriptors: "DEF (Diesel Exhaust Fluid)", "AUS 32", "urea solution 32.5% per ISO 22241 / IS 17042". ⚠️ Even phrases like "AdBlue equivalent" are legally grey — recommend "meets the same ISO 22241-1 specification required of AdBlue®" only with legal review, or avoid the word entirely.

## 2. Emission Norms Driving DEF Demand (India)

### On-road: BS-VI
- **BS-VI effective 1 April 2020 nationwide** (skipped BS-V). Diesel sulphur cut to 10 ppm.
- Heavy-duty BS-VI limits are Euro VI-aligned: **NOx 0.40 g/kWh (WHSC) / 0.46 g/kWh (WHTC)** vs BS-IV 3.5 g/kWh ⇒ ~87–89% NOx cut (sources quote "88.5% reduction" for HD diesel). PM 0.01 g/kWh; NH3 slip limit 10 ppm. ⚠️ dieselnet extraction was garbled; the 0.40/0.46 figures are the standard Euro VI-equivalent values — safe to publish as "BS-VI cuts NOx by ~90% vs BS-IV".
- Practically **all BS-VI heavy/medium diesel CVs and many diesel cars use SCR and therefore consume DEF**. OBD Phase-II (Apr 2023) + in-service PEMS testing tightened real-world compliance — vehicles derate/limp if DEF runs out or is poor quality.

### Agriculture: TREM (tractors)
- **TREM Stage IV: in force since 1 January 2023 for tractors >50 HP (~37 kW)**. Sub-50 HP tractors remain on TREM Stage III-A today. TREM IV drives electronic injection + after-treatment; SCR is a common route, especially >56 kW (NOx limit 0.4 g/kWh for 56–130 kW band). NH3 limit for SCR engines: 25 ppm (≤56 kW), 10 ppm (>56 kW).
- **TREM Stage V:** repeatedly delayed. Notified target was April 2026; **a Feb 2026 draft proposal staggers it: >75 HP (>56 kW) and <25 HP (<19 kW) from 1 October 2026; the 25–75 HP mass segment deferred, with full TREM V only by April 2032** (a mid-stage standard is under discussion). ⚠️ Still draft at research date — website should say "TREM IV today, TREM V being phased in from 2026", not hard dates.
- Stage V adds PN limits ⇒ DPF+SCR packages on affected bands.

### Construction equipment / harvesters: CEV
- **CEV Stage IV: effective October 2021** (37–560 kW). **CEV Stage V: engines >56 kW from ~2025/Oct 2026 per amendments; smaller categories already covered** — sources conflict on exact CEV-V dates after multiple deferrals (originally Apr 2024 → Jan 2025 → later). ⚠️ Verify current CEV-V date with MoRTH/CMVR before publishing.
- **Which agri/off-road machines need DEF in India today:** tractors >50 HP with SCR (Mahindra, John Deere, Sonalika, Massey Ferguson, New Holland all sell TREM-IV models), combine harvesters (typically >75 HP, TREM/CEV IV), CEV-IV/V construction machines >56 kW (backhoe loaders, excavators, cranes, loaders), and BS-VI commercial vehicles used in agri logistics.
- **Gensets (bonus segment): CPCB IV+ norms effective 1 July 2023** for new DG sets up to 800 kW — require after-treatment (DOC/DPF/**SCR**) with monitoring of DEF usage ⇒ genset owners are a genuine DEF buyer segment.

## 4. Market Context

- **India DEF market size:** BlueWeave: **USD 427.12 M (2023), CAGR 6.24% (2024–2030) → USD 693.65 M by 2030**. Other firms differ widely (MRFR shows an implausible −9.49% CAGR outlier). ⚠️ All market-size figures are paid-report estimates — publish as "estimated ~USD 400–450 M, growing 6–8%/yr" or avoid exact numbers.
- Growth drivers cited: BS-VI fleet accumulation post-Apr 2020, infra/logistics growth, TREM IV tractors, CPCB IV+ gensets, low production cost, agri-equipment adoption.
- **Consumption rate:** commercial vehicles typically consume DEF at **3–6% of diesel consumption** (Yara India says 4–6%; several sources 3–5%; up to ~8-10% in high-load Euro VI/BS-VI duty cycles). Rule of thumb for site copy: ~5 L DEF per 100 L diesel for BS-VI trucks.
- **Freezing:** freezes at **−11°C** (eutectic). Freezing/thawing does **not** degrade quality — fluid is fully usable after thaw. Expands ~7% on freezing (leave headspace in packs). Largely a non-issue in most of India except high Himalayan regions.
- **Shelf life (ISO 22241-3, Table on storage temp):** ≤10°C → 36 months; ≤25°C → 18 months; ≤30°C → 12 months; ≤35°C → 6 months; >35°C → test each batch before use / significant quality loss. Confirmed from multiple sources: 18 mo @ ≤25°C, 12 mo @ ≤30°C, 6 mo @ 35°C; ⚠️ the 36-mo @ ≤10°C row is widely cited (BASF TDS) but I couldn't read the primary table — Indian-market safe claim: **"shelf life 12 months under typical Indian storage (≤30°C, out of sunlight)"**.

## 5. Pack Sizes & Buyer Segments (India)

**Pack sizes in the Indian market:** 5 L, 10 L, 20 L cans/buckets (retail, single trucks, tractors); 50 L; **210 L drums** (small fleets, dealerships, workshops — one drum ≈ ~12,500 km of truck running); **1000 L IBC totes** (small–medium fleets ~25,000 L/yr, refill services offered at customer site); **bulk tanker/loose supply** (large fleets, OEM plants, fuel-pump storage). Pallet norms: 150×5 L, 90×10 L, 40×20 L.

**Buyer segments:** truck/bus fleet operators & transporters; OEM vehicle dealerships & authorised service centres; fuel pumps (IOCL/BPCL/HPCL retail DEF at forecourts — e.g. IOCL "ClearBlue"); agri-equipment dealers & harvester/tractor owners (>50 HP TREM IV); construction/mining equipment operators (CEV IV/V); **DG-set owners/facility managers (CPCB IV+, since Jul 2023)**; distributors/retail auto-parts shops. ⚠️ "ClearBlue" brand name — verify current IOCL branding before naming competitors on site.

## 6. Handling / Storage / Dispensing Best Practices (publishable content)

- **Purity is everything:** SCR catalysts are poisoned by ppm-level metals. Never top up with tap water, fertilizer-grade urea solution, or diesel. Even a teaspoon of contaminant can spoil a tank; contamination triggers vehicle derate/OBD faults.
- **Compatible materials:** HDPE / polypropylene / polyethylene, stainless steel 304/316, fiberglass (FRP), rubber-lined carbon steel; seals in EPDM or FKM (Viton).
- **Materials to AVOID:** copper, brass, zinc, aluminium, mild/carbon steel (unlined), nickel coatings, PVC, Buna-N (nitrile) seals — they corrode/leach ions that catalyse urea decomposition and poison the catalyst.
- **Dedicated equipment only:** pumps, hoses, funnels, nozzles used for DEF must never touch any other fluid. Rinse equipment with deionised water or fresh DEF (never tap water — mineral residue).
- **Storage:** sealed containers, cool, dry, out of direct sunlight; ideal −11°C to +30°C; avoid prolonged storage/transport above 25–30°C (urea decomposes → ammonia; vented containers lose water → concentration drifts out of spec). Keep containers closed to prevent dust ingress and evaporation.
- **Spills:** non-hazardous, non-flammable; rinse with water; corrosive to some metals/concrete over time; slippery when dry (crystallises).
- **Quality assurance talking points:** batch-wise lab testing to IS 17042 (Part 2)/ISO 22241-2, refractometer/density checks, ISI mark, sealed tamper-evident packs, manufacture + expiry date printed per storage-temperature table.
